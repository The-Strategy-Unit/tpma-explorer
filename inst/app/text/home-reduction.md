How much hospital activity can be reduced?

While the activity shown in the tool has been classified as 'potentially mitigatable', no one would suggest that **all** such activity can or should be mitigated.

The judgement of what proportion of the activity could be reduced is one that should be informed by the **knowledge and experience** of those involved, alongside **available evidence**.

The tool supports this judgement in two ways:

1. By helping local systems explore variation in hospital utilisation through **comparison** with statistical peers and the national average.
1. By showing the results of an **expert elicitation exercise**. Subject matter experts predicted how much activity could be mitigated nationally by 2039/40. The results provide a useful 'outside' view but should not be interpreted as an expectation of what can be achieved locally.
